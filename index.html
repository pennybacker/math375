Overview

This course is meant as an introduction to numerical analysis. We will study numerical methods used to solve linear and nonlinear equations, to interpolate and approximate data, and methods for numerical integration and differentiation. We will implement many of these algorithms in Matlab, and begin the course with a brief Matlab tutorial.

Schedule

0.5 Weeks Introduction to Numerical Analysis: Motivation and significance of numerical computation in science and engineering
1 Week Matlab Tutorial: Scalars, vectors, matrices, operations, built-in commands and functions, graphics, control structures, program organization, and effciency
2 Weeks Solving Nonlinear Equations: Iterative methods, including the bisection method, fixed-point iteration, Newtonâ€™s method, and the secant method
1 Week Finite Precision Effects, Conditioning, and Stability: Integer and floating point representation, machine epsilon, rounding errors and their effects
1.5 Weeks Direct Methods for Linear Systems: Tridiagonal systems, triangular systems, Gaussian elimination, LU factorization, PLU factorization, operation counts, error, residual, norms, and conditioning
1 Week Iterative Methods for Linear Systems: Jacobi method, Gauss-Seidel method, and sparse systems
1 Week Approximating Data: Overdetermined linear systems, least squares, normal equations, and QR factorization
2 Weeks Interpolation: Polynomial interpolation (Vandermonde, Lagrange, Newton), interpolation error, Runge phenomena, Chebyshev points, splines, and trigonometric interpolation
2 Weeks Numerical Differentiation and Integration: Numerical differentiation, Trapezoid rule, Newton-Cotes rules and Gaussian quadrature
2 Weeks Initial Value Problems: Forward Euler, convergence and stability, backward Euler and Runge-Kutta methods
